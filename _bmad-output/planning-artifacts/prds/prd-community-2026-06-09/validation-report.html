AceFluency Practice-English-with-Peers Rebuild — Validation Report

prd-community-2026-06-09/prd.md + addendum.md
Good — 6/7 Strong

This is a strong rebuild PRD. Every feature connects to a named user complaint, the user journeys are drawn from real Play Store reviews, and the counter-metrics show awareness of how incentive misalignment destroys the product. The rubric review rates 6 of 7 dimensions strong and 1 (done-ness clarity) adequate.

The adversarial review identifies three systemic risks the rubric review's dimension-by-dimension walk would not catch: (1) the aggregate scope is a new product, not a patch — 20 FRs across six feature areas with no timeline, team size, or phasing; (2) success metrics are disconnected from the user journeys that justify the rebuild; and (3) the India market is acknowledged but treated as a bolt-on afterthought, creating a silent second product surface. These three issues must be resolved before this PRD can drive architecture and story creation.

Combined finding counts: 3 critical, 8 high, 12 medium, 11 low. The critical and high findings cluster around scope boundaries (unbounded MVP, India market ambiguity), metric gaps (UJ validation, retention), and specification vagueness (Skip button, FR-20 threshold contradiction, crash/disconnect definitions).

Decision-readiness

Strong

The PRD is structured for a decision-maker who needs to say "go" or "no-go" on a rebuild. §0 names phase-blockers and their resolution status. OQ-6 (LiveKit Cloud vs. self-hosted) is resolved inline with rationale. OQ-1 (payment provider) is resolved with a named provider and API endpoint. OQ-2 (refund thresholds) is explicitly demoted from phase-blocker with honest reasoning.

Trade-offs are surfaced where they matter: the rejected-alternative section in addendum §A shows two paths considered and discarded with reasons. The non-goals in §5 do actual exclusion work.

Strategic coherence

Strong

The PRD has a clear thesis: the product loses users because it punishes them for infrastructure failures, and the rebuild's job is to make every failure mode visible, honest, and non-punishing. Every feature group maps to this thesis. Counter-metrics are the strongest coherence signal — SM-C1 directly names the Goodhart's Law failure the current product already exhibits.

§7
The thesis is "trust recovery" but there is no metric that measures trust directly. SM-2 (store rating) is a lagging proxy. The rebuild lacks a leading indicator of whether churned users return or new churn is prevented.
Fix: Add one retention-focused SM that measures behavior after a failure event (reconnect, strike warning, refund denial) to validate the "honest failure handling" thesis.

Done-ness clarity

Adequate

Most FRs have testable consequences in the "[Subject] can [action] [context] [outcome]" format. The weak spots are in billing and moderation features, where the PRD correctly identifies thresholds as "tuning parameters" but leaves some FRs without a testable v1 definition.

High

FR-20 "critical app crash" undefined

§4.5 FR-20, line 335
What counts as a "critical app crash"? The addendum §B adds "force-close or black-screen" but this clarification lives in the addendum, not the FR itself. An engineer implementing the crash-count trigger needs to know what telemetry event to count.
Fix: Add a parenthetical in FR-20's consequence: "critical app crash (force-close or ANR reported by the OS crash-reporting SDK)." Cross-ref addendum §B explicitly.
High

FR-11 "short disconnect" ambiguous — wall-clock vs. speech time

§4.4 FR-11, line 265
"Short disconnect = <30s of audio before end" is ambiguous: wall-clock time, active speech, or call duration? For a user matched with a silent partner (UJ-1), the call duration could be 90s but audio content 0s. The distinction determines whether Sumit's scenario triggers a strike.
Fix: Define "short disconnect" as call duration <30s (wall-clock time from both participants joining to one disconnecting). Note that silence-detection-based tuning is v2.
Medium

FR-17 backgrounding vs. OS-kill not distinguished

§4.6 FR-17, line 358
The FR says "up to 30 minutes" but does not distinguish between "app is backgrounded but alive" and "app was killed by the OS while backgrounded" — a common event on low-RAM Android devices (the primary market).
Fix: Split the consequence: (a) backgrounded-but-alive: call persists; (b) OS-killed while backgrounded: call ends, user returns to "Call ended — connection lost" state, no strike assessed.
Medium

FR-3 OTP fallback timeout undefined

§4.1 FR-3, line 155
"OTP delivery uses an SMS provider with a fallback to voice call for unreachable numbers." "Unreachable" is undefined — after how many failed SMS attempts? What timeout?
Fix: Add: "If SMS delivery is not confirmed within 60s, offer voice-call fallback."
Low

§4.1 FR-4

Fix: Add: "If Google OAuth fails, the user sees an error message with a 'Try again' button and a fallback to email/password login."

Adversarial review

review-adversarial.md

This PRD carries three systemic risks a rubric review would not surface: (1) it scopes a full billing/refund/moderation system from scratch inside a "rebuild" that also rewires auth, calls, and matching — the aggregate scope is a new product, not a patch; (2) its success metrics are disconnected from the user journeys that justify the rebuild; and (3) the India market is acknowledged in screenshots and pricing but treated as a bolt-on afterthought.

Critical

Scope is unbounded — no timeline, no team size, no phasing

§0, §6
The PRD defines 20 FRs spanning six feature areas including two greenfield subsystems, but never once states a delivery timeline, team size, or phase boundary. §6 "MVP Scope" includes every single FR. If everything is MVP, nothing is MVP.
Fix: Define a hard phase-1 that ships in ≤8 weeks with ≤5 FRs (e.g., auth + call reliability only), and a phase-2 for billing/moderation. State team size and a date or sprint count.
Critical

India market silently in scope but unscoped

§4.5, OQ-9, Addendum §H
The live app serves India with INR pricing and Indian tutors. But no FR addresses India-specific requirements: UPI payment flows, GST invoicing, RBI auto-debit mandate. The refund matrix in Addendum §B is "written for SSLCommerz (BDT)" with a hand-wave for India.
Fix: Explicitly declare India in-scope or out-of-scope for MVP. If in, add FRs for UPI, e-mandate compliance, GST invoicing. If out, remove INR references.
Critical

Success metrics do not validate the user journeys

§7 vs. §2.3
UJ-1 (Sumit) — no metric for false-positive strike rate. UJ-4 (Hari) — SM-9 allows 10% violation rate on a paid feature. UJ-5 (Mridu) — no SM measures successful cancellation outcome. The team can hit every SM and still not fix the problems that motivated the rebuild.
Fix: Add SM for false-positive moderation rate (target ≤1%), paid-filter violation rate (target 0%), and cancellation completion rate linked to actual billing outcome.
High

Skip button has no FR — "load-bearing" with zero acceptance criteria

§4.4 FR-11, Addendum §C
FR-11 says "Skip is a first-class in-call action… does not count as a strike." Addendum §C says "The Skip button is the load-bearing piece." But the PRD defers Skip to bmad-ux downstream with no FR, no acceptance criteria, no behavior spec. If bmad-ux deprioritizes or redesigns Skip, the entire moderation system collapses.
Fix: Promote Skip to a full FR with testable consequences: what happens to the current call, does the user re-enter the queue automatically, is there a cooldown between Skips, does the skipped partner get notified?
High

FR-20 refund auto-deny rule is self-contradictory

§4.5 FR-20 vs. Addendum §B
FR-20 states auto-deny triggers include "account has >5 completed sessions." Addendum §B states auto-deny triggers include "≥1 completed session." Under the addendum rule, Mridu's refund (UJ-5) would be denied — exactly the failure mode the PRD is trying to fix.
Fix: Reconcile. Auto-deny only if >5 completed sessions AND refund requested >14 days after charge (both conditions).
High

No FR for post-call rating flow

§2.3, OQ-7, Addendum §D, §H
The addendum confirms the post-call rating flow exists in the live app, OQ-7 says the rebuild "must preserve this flow," and Addendum §D lists five quality inputs that depend on it. But there is no FR — it is an orphaned dependency.
Fix: Add FR-21 for post-call rating: preserve existing flow, add "Did this partner help you practice?" question, feed rating into matchPartners scoring, define retention policy.
High

Existing features unaddressed for regression

§5, Addendum §H
The live app has 6 call types, tutor marketplace, AI speaking tests, recorded courses, drama tab, rooms/voice clubs, daily speaking streaks, a friend graph, and communities. Every one depends on auth, calls, or moderation being rebuilt. No regression scope is defined.
Fix: Add §4.7 "Regression Scope" FR that lists which existing features must continue working unchanged and defines acceptance tests for them.
High

SSLCommerz refund API failure has no fallback besides 7-day SLA

§4.5 FR-20
A SSLCommerz API outage converts every auto-approve refund into a human-review case with a 7-day SLA, with no user communication about the delay.
Fix: Define a retry policy (3 retries over 24h). Add a user-visible state: "Refund approved — processing with payment provider."
Medium

SM-2 baseline is fabricated

§7, §9
SM-2 says the current rating is "2.0–3.5 in sampled reviews" and targets ≥4.0. §9 says "the actual store rating is unknown." The PRD set a success metric against a baseline it does not know.
Fix: Measure the actual current Play Store rating before finalizing SM-2.
Medium

FR-3 phone OTP has no cost model or abuse mitigation

§4.1 FR-3
OTP via SMS in Bangladesh and India is not free. No FR addresses rate-limiting OTP requests, CAPTCHA before OTP send, cost ceiling per user, or abuse prevention.
Fix: Add an FR or NFR for OTP rate-limiting (max 5 OTP requests per phone per hour) and require architecture to define the cost model.
Medium

"Human review" appears in three places with no staffing definition

§4.4 FR-11, §4.5 FR-16, §4.5 FR-20
Three separate systems require human review, but the PRD never states: how many humans, what tools they use, what their working hours are, or whether the team has committed to staffing this.
Fix: Add a cross-cutting organizational dependency section. State: "This PRD requires N support/moderation staff or an AI triage system."
Medium

SM-9 gender filter fulfillment at 90% formalizes partial failure

§7 SM-9
A user who pays for gender filtering and gets a wrong-gender match 10% of the time is experiencing UJ-4's exact failure. SM-9 at 90% is not success; it is formalized partial failure.
Fix: Redefine: 0% of matches should violate the filter. Separately measure "% of gender-filtered queue sessions that result in no match within 90s."
Medium

No accessibility requirements anywhere

§4, §6
The PRD targets South Asian users on low-end Android devices. No FR mentions text size, contrast ratios, screen reader support, RTL text support (Urdu is listed), or localization.
Fix: Add a cross-cutting NFR for WCAG 2.1 AA compliance on primary flows, and an FR for UI localization (English, Bangla, Hindi).
Medium

FR-8 empty-pool fallback creates a dark pattern risk

§4.3 FR-8
If the pool is consistently empty for a gender preference, the user will be asked to drop their paid filter every single session — a de facto upsell-for-nothing.
Fix: Add a metric for "% of gender-filtered sessions that resolve without fallback prompt." If below 50% for 7 consecutive days, trigger a PM review.
Medium

Addendum §B auto-deny for "≥1 completed session" is hostile to users

Addendum §B
Under this rule, a user who completed one 5-minute call then experienced 6 days of crashes would be auto-denied a refund — the opposite of the PRD's stated philosophy.
Fix: Raise auto-deny threshold to ≥5 completed sessions, or make conditional on absence of reported technical issues.
Low

CEFR placement accuracy never validated

§3, §4.3
If CEFR placement is inaccurate (self-reported), the matching system is filtering on noise regardless of new filter infrastructure.
Fix: Acknowledge as known risk and add a v1 metric: "% of post-call ratings where user selected 'partner was too advanced/too basic'."
Low

FR-12 report-voids-strike is exploitable

§4.4 FR-12
A user who wants to serial-disconnect without consequence can report every partner for "non-participation" within 60s. No false-report abuse mitigation.
Fix: If a user reports >50% of partners in a 24h window, flag the user for review instead of voiding all strikes.
Low

No data migration plan for existing users

§4.5, §4.4
The rebuild adds new schema fields and changes auth behavior. No FR addresses how existing users get new fields populated or how existing subscriptions are reconciled.
Fix: Add an FR or architecture note for data migration: existing users get a one-time onboarding prompt for new fields.
Low

"Pronunciation Score" glossary ghost

§3
Glossary defines "Pronunciation Score" but no FR references it. No SM validates it. It exists in the spec but has no functional home.
Fix: Remove from glossary or add to §6.2 Out of Scope with a note that it is preserved as-is.
Low

No error/edge-case handling for dual-PSP billing

§4.5, OQ-9
No FR addresses what happens if a user switches countries, how currency is determined, or how billing history displays cross-currency transactions.
Fix: Add an FR or architecture constraint: currency is determined at purchase time and locked for the billing period.
